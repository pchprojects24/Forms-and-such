This is a field-friendly operational guide. Always follow your unit policy and the ZOLL operator materials for definitive instructions.

Monitoring only (ECG / SpO₂ / NIBP / EtCO₂)

When to use

  • Any patient requiring monitoring without immediate shock or pacing.
  • Transport / observation / procedural monitoring.

Controls to recognize

  • ON/OFF selector: rotary control (turn from OFF to MONITOR/DEFIB depending on your unit).
  • Navigation knob: rotary/push knob to select menus and adjust values.
  • Soft keys / quick-access keys: screen-adjacent keys for common functions (Lead, Alarms, NIBP, Print, Setup—varies).
  • NIBP start/stop: dedicated button or soft-key depending on configuration.

Step-by-step (basic monitoring)

  1. Power: insert a charged SurePower II battery (or connect external power if available), then turn ON/OFF selector to power on.
  2. ECG: connect ECG cable → apply electrodes to clean/dry skin → select lead/display as needed.
  3. SpO₂: plug in sensor → apply to well-perfused site → confirm waveform/number stabilizes.
  4. NIBP: select correct cuff size → connect hose → apply snug to bare limb at heart level → press NIBP start.
  5. EtCO₂ (if equipped): connect line/module → attach to airway adapter/cannula → confirm waveform.
  6. Alarms: open Alarm Limits → confirm patient category (adult/ped/neo if used) → set limits appropriate to patient.
Critical checks: confirm correct patient category/alarm defaults; confirm clean ECG waveform (replace electrodes if noisy); fix NIBP errors by re-positioning cuff/limb before repeating; confirm adequate battery for transport.
Post-use: stop NIBP cycling; disconnect disposables per infection control; wipe device per approved cleaning guidance.
Manual defibrillation (VF / pulseless VT — manual control)

When to use

  • VF / pulseless VT when operating in manual mode per your resuscitation workflow.

Controls to recognize

  • Energy select: soft key + navigation knob (varies by configuration).
  • CHARGE: button/soft key (varies).
  • SHOCK: guarded orange button (front panel) or on therapy control (config-dependent).
  • Pads/therapy connection: ensure multifunction pads/cable are connected and recognized.

Step-by-step (manual shock)

  1. Mode: turn selector to DEFIB/manual therapy mode (as labeled on your unit).
  2. Pads: apply approved ZOLL multifunction pads (anterior-lateral or AP as appropriate) and connect therapy cable.
  3. Rhythm check: confirm shockable rhythm per protocol.
  4. Select energy: set energy per local protocol.
  5. Charge: press CHARGE and wait for “charged” indicator/tone.
  6. Clear: ensure no one is touching patient/bed/rails; announce and verify “all clear.”
  7. Shock: press and hold SHOCK until delivered.
  8. Immediately resume CPR and follow your rhythm-check cycle.
Critical checks: confirm pads contact/impedance is acceptable (no “check pads”); confirm you are NOT in SYNC for VF/pulseless VT; if charge times out/disarms, re-charge before attempting shock.
Synchronized cardioversion (organized rhythm with pulse)

When to use

  • Unstable tachyarrhythmias with an organized QRS where synchronized shock is indicated.

Controls to recognize

  • SYNC: soft key/button enabling synchronization.
  • R-wave markers: on-screen indicators that must align with QRS complexes.

Step-by-step (sync cardioversion)

  1. Mode: enter DEFIB/manual therapy mode.
  2. Pads: ensure pads connected; ensure ECG trace is clean and QRS is obvious.
  3. Enable SYNC: press SYNC and confirm SYNC indicator + markers over each R wave.
  4. Select energy: per local protocol.
  5. Charge: press CHARGE and confirm “charged.”
  6. Clear: “all clear” check.
  7. Shock: press and hold SHOCK; device delivers on next appropriate R wave.
  8. Reassess: rhythm + patient status; repeat as clinically indicated.
Critical checks: SYNC may turn off after shock or mode change—confirm SYNC before each attempt. Ensure markers align with QRS (not T waves); adjust lead/gain/filter if needed.
Transcutaneous pacing (TCP)

When to use

  • Symptomatic bradycardia with poor perfusion when pacing is indicated per your protocols.

Controls to recognize

  • PACE: pacing mode selection.
  • RATE and mA (current): adjusted via soft keys + navigation knob.
  • Start/Stop pacing: on-screen control in pacing panel (varies).

Step-by-step (TCP)

  1. Pads + ECG: ensure multifunction pads connected and ECG monitoring is active.
  2. Enter pacing mode: press PACE to open pacing controls.
  3. Set rate: select pacing rate per local protocol.
  4. Set current: start at a reasonable initial output (per local practice) and be prepared to increase.
  5. Start pacing: start pacing and observe pacing spikes on ECG.
  6. Increase to capture: increase mA until electrical capture then confirm mechanical capture (pulse/BP response).
  7. Monitor: reassess capture and patient response; document thresholds per policy.
Critical checks: confirm mechanical capture (not just spikes/QRS). Poor pad contact/impedance can prevent capture. TCP is often uncomfortable—manage analgesia/sedation per your local practice.
Power & SurePower™ II battery management

Power on/off

  1. Insert SurePower II battery until it seats/locks.
  2. Turn selector from OFF to ON/MONITOR/DEFIB (label varies) and allow self-test to complete.
  3. To power off, ensure patient is safely transferred/alternate monitoring arranged, then turn selector back to OFF.

Battery status (on the battery pack)

  • Use the battery’s display button to show LED runtime bars (and any fault/recalibration indicators).
  • Before transport/mission: verify at least one good battery (no fault indicator) and adequate runtime indication.
Common failure mode: deploying with a weak/unmaintained battery → low-battery alarms or unexpected shutdown. Make battery checks part of routine readiness.
Do’s & Don’ts

Do

  • Do confirm patient category/alarm limits at start of case (adult/ped/neo if used).
  • Do ensure pads/leads/sensors are firmly connected and waveforms are clean before acting on them.
  • Do verify “RUNNING/CHARGED/SYNC” indicators on-screen (don’t assume).
  • Do check battery status before transport and keep a spare available when possible.

Don’t

  • Don’t cardiovert without confirming SYNC markers over R waves.
  • Don’t deliver therapy with “check pads/leads off” unresolved.
  • Don’t assume pacing spikes = perfusion; confirm mechanical capture.
  • Don’t ignore repeated NIBP errors—reapply cuff/limb or change site.
Common pitfalls (fast troubleshooting cues)

Lead / cable problems

  • Noisy ECG: dry/loose electrodes, poor skin prep, movement — replace electrodes and re-prep skin.
  • Intermittent SpO₂: low perfusion, motion, poor sensor fit — reposition and warm limb if possible.

SYNC errors

  • SYNC turned off after shock/mode change — confirm before each cardioversion attempt.
  • Markers on T waves or inconsistent — change lead, adjust gain/filter, improve electrode contact.

Charge / shock delivery issues

  • Energy disarms after delay — re-confirm “charged” and re-charge if needed.
  • High impedance (“check pads”) — re-seat/replace pads, dry skin, remove heavy hair if needed.

Battery failures

  • Unexpected low battery — verify battery LEDs before use; rotate/maintain packs per your local schedule.
